Forkinit: Yellow Squash Casserole

By Craig Cottingham

Ingredients

1 lb yellow crookneck squash, sliced lengthwise into quarters, then crosswise into 1/2" wedges
1/4 C carrots, peeled and coarsely chopped
1 tsp kosher salt

2 tbsp unsalted butter
1/2 medium yellow onion, chopped
1 small garlic clove, pressed

1 tbsp unsalted butter
1 C Ritz cracker crumbs (about 25 crackers)
1 tsp chopped fresh thyme (optional)

1/2 C grated mild or medium Cheddar cheese
2 tsp chopped fresh thyme (optional)
Tabasco to taste
salt and freshly ground black pepper
1 large egg, lightly beaten

Instructions

Place squash and carrots in saucepan and barely cover with water; add salt. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, cover and simmer until just tender, about 5 minutes. Drain well and plunge into ice water bath to cool. Drain well again and lay out on paper towels to dry.

Preheat oven to 350°F. Spray a 1-qt baking dish with Pam.

Warm the 2 tbsp butter in a skillet over medium-low heat. Stir in onions and cook slowly until very soft and translucent, 6 to 8 minutes; do not brown. Add garlic and sauté another minute. Scrape into large bowl. Wipe out skillet, melt the remaining butter and stir in cracker crumbs; cook briefly until crumbs are golden; add the thyme. Scrape crumbs onto a small plate and reserve.

Add squash mixture to bowl with onions. Add 1/4 to 1/2 C of the cracker crumbs, thyme, cheese and Tabasco. Season to taste with salt and pepper. Stir in egg and spoon into prepared baking dish. Scatter remaining crumbs over the top and bake uncovered 20 minutes or until golden brown and slightly firm in center.

Yields: 2
Source: Adapted from Cheryl & Bill Jamison's "American Home Cooking"
