Forkinit: Shrimp and Grits Lowcountry Style

By Craig Cottingham

Ingredients


Grits:
1 1/2 C chicken broth
1/2 tbsp butter
1/8 tsp salt
1/4 C plus 2 tbsp quick grits
2 tbsp cream cheese
1 tbsp half and half
1/4 C chopped green onions

Shrimp:
1/2 lb shrimp, peeled, deveined, and patted dry
1 tbsp vegetable oil
1/8 tsp salt
1/8 tsp black pepper
pinch sugar
1/8 tsp Cajun seasoning

Sauce:
1/2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
Cajun seasoning to taste
1 tbsp bacon grease
1/4 C finely chopped onion
1/4 C finely chopped green pepper
2 tbsp flour
3/4 C hot chicken broth or water, more if necessary

Instructions


Grits:
Combine broth, butter, and salt in a heavy saucepan and bring to a boil.
Stir in grits, reduce heat, cover and simmer 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
Mix cream cheese and half and half into grits.
Cover and simmer until almost all liquid has evaporated and grits are tender, stirring frequently, about 7 minutes.
Stir in green onions and set aside.

Shrimp:
In a bowl, toss shrimp with salt, pepper, sugar, and Cajun seasoning. Set aside.
Heat 1 tbsp vegetable oil in a skillet until smoking.
Add shrimp to skillet in a single layer and cook until spotty brown and edges turn pink, about 1 minute. (If all shrimp won’t fit in skillet in a single layer, do two batches.)
Remove pan from heat; using tongs, turn each shrimp over and let stand until all but very center is opaque, about 30 seconds.
Transfer to bowl and let stand until cooked through, about 1 to 2 minutes.

Sauce:
Heat bacon grease in skillet and sauté onion and green pepper over medium heat until onions become transparent.
Sprinkle flour over vegetables and stir constantly, until flour begins to brown.
Add 3/4 C hot broth or water, stirring constantly until sauce thickens.
Add lemon juice and additional Cajun seasoning to taste.
Return shrimp to skillet and cook for 1-2 minutes, until shrimp are warm and gravy is smooth; thin with a little extra liquid if necessary.

Serve:
Spoon grits onto plates or into shallow bowls and top with shrimp mixture.

Yields: 2
Source: Mom & Dad; adapted from South Carolina Congressman Floyd D. Spence’s recipe and Folly Island Shrimp and Grits recipe
