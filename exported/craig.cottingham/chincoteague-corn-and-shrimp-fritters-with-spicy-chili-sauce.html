Forkinit: Chincoteague Corn and Shrimp Fritters with Spicy Chili Sauce

By Craig Cottingham

Ingredients


For fritters:
3/4 C flour
1 1/2 tsp baking powder
1 tsp Old Bay® seasoning
1/2 tsp sweet paprika
2 eggs, beaten
1/4 C half-and-half
1/4 C clam juice
2 tbsp red bell pepper, very finely chopped
1 C shrimp, cooked and coarsely chopped
1/2 C cooked corn kernels, fresh off the cob
vegetable oil, for frying
confectioners sugar
chili sauce

For chili sauce:
1 C water
1 C malt vinegar
1 C sugar
2 tsp fresh ginger root, minced
1 tsp garlic, minced
1 tsp sweet onion, minced
1 tbsp hot chile pepper, minced
drop or two worcestershire sauce
2 tsp ketchup
2 tsp cornstarch

Instructions


For fritters:
In a large bowl, mix flour, baking powder, paprika and Old Bay seasoning.
In a separate bowl, mix eggs, clam juice, half-and-half and red bell pepper.
Combine mixtures, blending until moistened. Fold in cooked shrimp and corn.
Pour oil into heavy skillet to a depth of 1 inch. Heat to 375°F on medium heat.
Drop batter by the tablespoonful, a few at a time, into hot oil. Fry 3 to 4 minutes or until golden brown.
Drain fritters on paper towels. Just before serving, lightly dust fritters with confectioners sugar.

For chili sauce:
Pour water and vinegar into a saucepan, and bring to a boil over high heat. Stir in sugar, ginger, garlic, sweet onion, chili pepper, Worcestershire sauce and ketchup; simmer for 5 minutes.
Stir in cornstarch. Remove saucepan from stove to cool. Then transfer to a bowl, cover, and refrigerate until needed.

Yields: 6
Prep time: 20 min
