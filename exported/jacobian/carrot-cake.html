Forkinit: Carrot cake

By Jacob Kaplan-Moss

Ingredients

Cake:

12 1/2 oz AP flour
1 1/4 t baking power
1 t baking soda
1 1/4 t cinnamon
1/2 t nutmeg
1/8 t cloves
1 1/2 t ground ginger
1/2 t salt
1 1/4 lb carrots, grated (easiest to do in a food processor)
1/2 c crystallized ginger, chopped very fine
10 1/2 oz sugar
3 1/2 oz brown sugar
4 eggs
1 T orange zest
1 1/2 c oil

Frosting:

8 oz cream cheese, *very cold*
5 t unsalted butter, softened
1 T sour cream
1/2 t vanilla
6 oz powdered sugar, sifted twice

Instructions

Prep:

1. Pre-heat oven to 350Â°.
2. Line a 13x9 baking pan with parchment.

Cake (modified muffin method):

1. Whisk together the dry ingredients in a large bowl.
2. Grate the carrots in a food processor with grating disk and set aside.
3 Process the sugar, eggs, and zest.
4. With the food processor running, add the oil in a steady stream; you're looking for an emulsion.
5. Add wet ingredients, carrots, and ginger to the dry and stir until just combined.
6. Bake 35-40 minutes; cool for 2 hours.

Frosting:

1. Cream the butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
2. Add the sour cream and vanilla.
3. Add the cheese a chunk at a time. If the cheese isn't totally cold, you'll get a goopy frosting.
If it starts to get warm, slap it in the fridge for a bit and come back later.

Yields: 1 13x9 baking pan
Source: Adapted from Baking Illustrated and Alton Brown's _I'm just here for more food_.
