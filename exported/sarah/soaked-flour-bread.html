Forkinit: Soaked Flour Bread

By Sarah Potter
Soaking the flour in raw apple cider vinegar helps make the wheat more easily digestible so this is great for people who have a slight wheat intolerance. Does not have a "sour" flavor when finished!

Ingredients

Mix together until dough forms a ball:
2 c. warm water
2 T raw apple cider vinegar
5-6 c. whole grain flour (wheat, spelt, kamut, etc. or combination)

After dough has soured, mix together:
1/3 c. warm water
1 beaten egg
1/2 t. baking soda
1/3 c. melted butter
1/3 c. honey
2 t. salt
1/8 t. ascorbic acid powder (optional)
1/2 c. flour mixed with 1 T. instant yeast

Instructions

Cover first mixture and let sit 12-24 hours.
Combine soured mixture and yeast mixture - dough should be sticky.
Begin adding more flour and kneading - dough should remain slightly soft at end of kneading.
Place dough into oiled bowl, turn to coat, let rise 2 hours.
Punch down and let rise again until double (second rising is faster than first)
Knead briefly and shape into three loaves. Place in buttered pans.
Let rise until double - do not over-rise.
Preheat oven to 450. Bake 10 min. Reduce heat to 350 and bake additional 20-30 min.
Immediately turn loaves onto cooling rack.
Enjoy with lots of butter!

Yields: 3 loaves
Prep time: 2 days
