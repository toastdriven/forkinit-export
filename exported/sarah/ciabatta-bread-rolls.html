Forkinit: Ciabatta Bread / Rolls

By Sarah Potter
Italian "slipper" bread using sourdough instead of a Biga.

Ingredients

2 cup sourdough starter
1 heaping tsp. yeast
1/4 cup milk
1 3/4 cup warm water
2-4 Tbs. olive oil
4 cups flour (can use mixture of white and whole wheat)
1 heaping Tbs. salt

Instructions

Use a happy room temperature sourdough starter, preferably fed the day before.
Combine all ingredients except salt and let rest 20 minutes.
Add salt and knead in mixer 7-15 minutes. Dough should look smooth, creamy and glossy when finished. It will be very soft and sticky.
Let rise in oiled bowl until tripled. Do not punch down the dough.
Turn dough onto a well floured surface.
Flour top of dough and very gently pat into an even thickness. You don't want to deflate the dough.
For loaves, cut into 4. Tri-fold to form a rectangle.
For rolls, cut into 2 inch squares (for 48 rolls) or preferred size.
Place formed dough on parchment sheets on a thin cookie sheet.
cover and let rise until almost doubled.
Preheat oven 45 minutes with baking stone inside. 425 F
Transfer the loaf directly to the stone to bake 20 minutes or until pale golden.
For rolls set the tray directly onto the stone to bake.

Yields: 4 loaves or 48 rolls
