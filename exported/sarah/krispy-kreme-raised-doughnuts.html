Forkinit: Krispy Kreme Raised Doughnuts

By Sarah Potter

Ingredients

7 teaspoons yeast
1/2 cup water
2 1/4 cup milk, scalded, then cooled
3/4 cup sugar
1 1/2 teaspoons salt
3 eggs
1/2 cup butter
7 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
lard for frying

Glaze:
1/2 cup butter
3 cups powdered sugar
2 1/4 teaspoon vanilla
6 to 9 tablespoons evaporated milk (can substitute milk, cream, or water)

Instructions

Proof your yeast by adding it to the warm water. Mix it up and let it rest.
Scald milk and let cool.
Combine yeast, milk, sugar, salt, egg, shortening and 3 cup flour. Beat on low or 30 seconds, scraping bowl constantly. Beat on medium speed for two minute, scraping bowl occasionally.
Cover and let rise until double, 30 to 60 minute.
After dough has risen, turn dough onto floured surface; roll around lightly to coat with flour. Gently roll dough 1/2 inch thick with floured rolling pin.
Cut with floured donut cutter.
Left rise until double, 30 to 40 minutes. (I let the scraps rise also, and fried them along with the doughnut holes.
Heat deep frying oil to 350Â°F.
Carefully place the donuts in the oil. Cook on each side for about one minute. Drain on racks or paper bags or paper towels.
Dip in glaze or ganache or powdered sugar or sugar and set aside to dry.

Glaze:
Make glaze while the doughnuts are rising.
melt butter and stir in powdered sugar and vanilla until smooth. Add milk until desired consistency is reached.
To make chocolate glaze, melt 6 ounces of chocolate along with the butter.

Yields: About 3 dozen
