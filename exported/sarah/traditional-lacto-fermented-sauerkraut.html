Forkinit: Traditional Lacto-Fermented Sauerkraut

By Sarah Potter

Ingredients

5 lbs. shredded cabbage (green or red or a combination) be sure to weigh AFTER shredding
3 T. canning salt
1 T. caraway seed (optional)

Instructions

Remove outer leaves and rinse cabbage.
Shred cabbage as for coleslaw or larger.
Put cabbage into the bowl and sprinkle with salt, mixing well to salt all of the cabbage.
Cover leave sit for at least an hour to draw the juices out.
Use a mallet to press the cabbage (do not pound to bruise the cabbage as it will get mushy)
Spoon into canning jars or gallon jars pressing down tightly as you go. The liquid should rise over the top of the cabbage. Leave at least 2 inches or more space at the top of the jar.
Mark the date (sharpie markers on glass work well) and set aside to ferment on the counter.
Check at least once per day to be sure cabbage is still submerged, if not, press down again.
Soon the contents will become bubbly and begin to smell like sauerkraut.
Depending on the climate, it may be done in as little as three days or up to a week or more (larger jars seem to take a little longer as well) The liquids will swell up, oftentimes overflowing the jars, and several days later subside back down. This indicates the main fermentation is done.
When cabbage is slightly soft but still crunchy and tastes delightfully sour, transfer jars to refrigerator where they will keep for many months - if they last that long! The flavor improves with age.
