Forkinit: Donuts

By Sarah Potter
So far, my kids favorite home-made donut.

Ingredients

1 c. warm milk
5 t. yeast (two packets)
pinch of sugar
2 c. flour
1/2 c. warm smashed potato (boil and smash with a fork)
1/2 t. salt
1/4 c. sugar
2 T. butter or oil
1/2 inch or deeper lard or oil in a frying pan or electric skillet set to 360

Instructions

Warm milk and add yeast and pinch of sugar. Let stand.
In a bowl combine flour, potato, salt and sugar.
Add milk mixture and oil to flour mixture.
Mix with a dough hook for 3 minutes.
Prepare your kneading surface by spreading a few tablespoons of flour on a smooth surface.
Put the dough in the middle of the flour and sprinkle a little flour on top and on your hands. Knead the dough a few times.
Once the dough has been kneaded, spread it out to be a half inch thick with your hands or with a rolling pin.
Cut out donuts and let rise 10 minutes while you heat up the oil.
Cook donuts till golden brown on each side.

Yields: 1 dozen donuts or 3 dozen donut holes
Prep time: 30 minutes
