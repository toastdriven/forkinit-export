Forkinit: Lebkuchen

By Heather
A traditional Xmas cookies from my husband's family. The batter makes a HUGE batch (12 cups of flour !!!). It is similar to a gingerbread cookie. The can be rolled into "logs" and frozen. The logs keeps for up to 6 months in the freezer so for one day of work, you get fresh cookies all winter.

Ingredients

2 c brown sugar
2 c lite molasses
1 c crisco
1 c milk
12 c flour
1 tsp bakeing soda
1 tsp salt
1 tbsp cinnamon
1 tsp cloves
1 tsp nutmeg
1 c black walnuts, chopped (optional)

Iceing

Instructions

Boil the brown sugar, light molasses and Crisco together and let cool. Watch it so that it doesn't boil over.

Sift/combine in a large bowl all the dry ingredients except the walnuts.

Put the cool brown sugar/molasses/crisco mix in a very large bowl. Slowly add the milk and dry mix, alternating a little from each. Mix well. It will form a Very stiff dough. Mix in nuts if using.

Chill dough.

It can be rolled out and use cookie cutters. Or roll into logs. The logs can be frozen and will keep in the freezer for 6 months. The logs can be taken out and cut into slices to make cookies.

Put on an ungreased pan.

Bake at 375 deg F for 7 to 10 min.

While the cookies are cooling, put some icing on them.

Yields: A lot !!!
