Forkinit: Lavash Crackers

By Heather
These are really yummy "Armenian-style" crackers. They are super easy to make.

Ingredients

1 1/2 cups bread flour (6.75 oz)
1/4 tsp salt (.06 oz)
1/2 tsp instant yeast (.055oz)
1 tbsp honey (.75 oz)
1 tbsp vegetable oil (.5 oz)
1/3 cups water, room temp (1/3 a cup to 1/2 cup) (3 to 4 oz)

Instructions

In a small bowl, mix together all the ingredients with just enough water to make it form a ball. I would not use a stand mixer.. Just use your hands or a spatula... It's not a big ball.
Sprinkle some flour on the counter, and kneed by have for about 10 min (until it's done). The dough will be a little firmer than standard bread dough.
Lightly oil a bowl, put the dough in. Cover the bowl.
Let it rise at room temp until it has doubled in size (about 90 min)
Preheat the oven to 350 deg F.
Roll the dough out (you will likely need some flour on the counter) until it's super thin.
Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Transfer the dough to the baking sheet.
If you want the crackers all the same size, cut the dough with a pizza cutter. If you want to just snap it by hand, don't cut it.
Bake for 15 to 20 min (until it is begins to brown evenly across the top).
Cool in the pan for 10 min.

Prep time: 2.5-3 hours
Source: "The Bread Baker's Apprentice" by Peter Reinhart
