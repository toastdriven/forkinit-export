Forkinit: Bacon fried rice

By kmonahan

Ingredients

1.5 cups (uncooked cups) of day/hours-old rice [Note: use slightly less (1/8-1/4 cup) water than you usually do when cooking. The later you cook the rice, the less water you use]
Package of frozen mixed veggies (1lb recommended, less ok)
2 tsp Ginger garlic paste
Strips of bacon (4-10, ~0.5lb, whatever you have), cut to small 1/4" pieces.
Eggs (3-6, whatever you have)
Optional: whatever veggie you have left over (4 stalks of celery chopped, or small onion chopped, or half a bag of spinach coarsely cut, no carrots)

Whatever spices you have, I suggest:
soy sauce
sugar
garlic powder
onion powder
white pepper
black pepper
sesame oil
anything chinese
never tried worcestershire but try it

Instructions

Crack the eggs in a bowl and add a few drops of soy sauce, a few drops of evoo, and around 1/8-1/4 tsp of salt. Whisk with fork.
Heat stove to med-hi and add small amount of evoo.
Scramble the egg to fingertip size.
Put the egg back to the egg-whisking bowl (no need to wash the bowl, you will re-cook the egg anyway)

Heat the stove again and heat bacon, without evoo.
When bacon browns and/or curls a little, add frozen veggies, ginger garlic paste and veggies
When veggies are done, turn heat to super low.
Add rice, using your hand to crumble the rice before adding. This saves you work later.
Use the spatula to break up the rice.
Add egg. Put heat back up to low/low-med.
Add all the spices and mix very well (write down whatever amount you use for later reference), keep tuning until you like it.
