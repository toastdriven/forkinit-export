Forkinit: Grilled Butter Chicken on a Bed of Watercress Mash

By joughrey
An Antony Worrall Thompson dish from Hello magazine.

Ingredients

700 g/ 1 1/2 waxy potatoes, peeled
2 85 g bags fresh watercress, roughly chopped
90 ml/ 3 3/4 fl oz 0% fat Greek yogurt or milk
25 g/ 1 oz unsalted butter, diced
Pinch of freshly grated nutmeg
salt and freshly ground black pepper
75 g/ 3 oz watercress butter (see below)
4 175 g/ 6 oz chicken breast fillets
1 TBSP olive oil
watercress sprigs
lemon wedges

For the Watercress Butter:
1 85 g fresh watercress, finely chopped.
75 g/ 3 oz unsalted butter, softened
1 shallot, peeled and very finely chopped.
1 tsp English mustard

Instructions

First make the watercress butter:
Place all the ingredients in a bowl and mix together with a fork. Season with plenty of freshly ground black pepper and reserve, keeping at room temperature.

Cut the potatoes into large chunks. Place in a pan of lightly salted water, cover with a lid and bring to a boil. Simmer for 10-15 minutes or until tender.

Drain, return to the pan and tip in the chopped watercress. REplace the lid and leave to stand for 1-2 minutes or until the watercress has wilted. Mash then add the yogurt or milk and butter. Fluff up with a fork, add the nutmeg and season to taste with ground black pepper. Reserve, keeping warm.

Meanwhile, gently push a little of the watercress butter under the skin of the chicken fillets.

Preheat a non-stick frying pan, and when hot, add the oil. Cook the chicken, skin-side down, for 10-15 minutes, turning once until the chicken is golden-brown on both sides and cooked through.

Heap the watercress mash in the center of four warmed plates and top with the chicken. Spoon the pan juices around and serve garnished with a sprig of watercress and a wedge of lemon.

Yields: 4 servings
Prep time: 30 minutes
