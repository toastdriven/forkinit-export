Forkinit: Thai Lemongrass-Coconut Noodle Soup

By Moriah Lindsley

Ingredients

2 stalks lemongrass (see complete instructions below), OR 4 T frozen prepared lemongrass
1 pkg Thai rice noodles
1 thumb-size piece galangal OR ginger, thinly sliced into matchstick-like pieces
4-6 C vegetable broth or vegetarian "chicken" broth (6 C serves 3-4 people)
1/2 pkg firm tofu, cubed, OR wheat gluten or soy chicken (or other meat substitute), cut into bite-size pieces
1 head broccoli, chopped into florets including stems
1-2 C Chinese cabbage, chopped into bite-size pieces (bok choy, baby bok choy or su choy)
1-2 carrots, sliced
2-3 T vegetarian fish sauce (OR substitute soy sauce)
2 T soy sauce (use wheat-free soy sauce for gluten-free diets)
1/2 can good-quality coconut milk
Roughly 1 C fresh basil, roughly chopped if leaves are large
Optional: Thai or Vietnamese chili sauce

Instructions

1. First, place noodles in a pot of boiling water. Cover the pot and turn off the heat. Allow the noodles to soak in the hot water while you prepare the soup.
2. Place broth in a soup pot along with lemongrass (include left-over stalk pieces), galangal or ginger, and tofu or soy/wheat gluten. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium. Allow to simmer while you chop up and add the vegetables (about 5 minutes).
3. Add the carrots first, then the broccoli and finally the cabbage. Stir and then allow to simmer 2 more minutes.
4. Add the coconut milk, stirring until dissolved. Reduce heat to minimum.
5. Add the vegetarian fish sauce and/or soy sauce. If you prefer your soup spicy, add 1-2 tsp chili sauce (OR simply serve it on the side for those who like it).
6. Do a taste test, adding more fish or soy sauce if not salty enough. If you find the soup too salty (this depends on how salty your broth is), add 1 to 2 T lime juice.
7. Check rice noodles to make sure they are cooked (if still a little hard, boil briefly). Drain the noodles and portion them out into bowls. If you have extra noodles left over, leave them in the colander and rinse with cold water to prevent sticking.
8. Pour several ladles of soup over each bowl of noodles. Sprinkle fresh basil over each bowl. Serve with chili sauce on the side (if desired), plus more fresh basil, and enjoy this bowl of culinary comfort.
