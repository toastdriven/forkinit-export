Forkinit: Taco-Filled Pasta Shells

By Moriah Lindsley
A modified version of Marge Hodel's meat-filled shells.

Ingredients

2 lbs TVP, seitan or tofu
2 envelopes taco seasoning
1 pkg (8 ounces) vegan cream cheese, cubed
24 uncooked jumbo pasta shells
1/4 C vegan butter or margarine, melted
Oil

ADDITIONAL INGREDIENTS (for each casserole):
1 C salsa
1 C taco sauce
1 C (4 ounces) vegan cheddar cheese, shredded
1 C (4 ounces) vegan monterey jack or mozzarella cheese, shredded
1-1/2 C crushed tortilla chips
1 C (8 ounces) vegan sour cream
3 green onions, chopped

Instructions

1. In a skillet, heat TVP, seitan or tofu in a little oil. Add taco seasoning; prepare according to package directions. Add vegan cream cheese; cover and simmer for 5-10 minutes or until melted. Transfer to a bowl; chill for 1 hour. Cook pasta according to package directions; drain. Gently toss with vegan butter. Fill each shell with about 3 tablespoons of the mixture.

2. Place 12 shells in a greased 9-in. square baking dish. Cover and freeze for up to 3 months. To prepare remaining shells, spoon salsa into a greased 9-in. square baking dish. Top with stuffed shells and taco sauce. Cover and bake at 350Ã‚Â°F for 30 minutes. Uncover; sprinkle with vegan cheeses and chips. Bake 15 minutes longer or until heated through. Serve with vegan sour cream and onions.

3. To use frozen shells: Thaw in the refrigerator for 24 hours (shells will be partially frozen). Remove from dish. Add salsa to dish; top with shells and taco sauce. Cover and bake at 350Ã‚Â°F for 40 minutes. Uncover and continue as above. Yield: 2 casseroles.
