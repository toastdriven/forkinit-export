Forkinit: Bryanna's Crispy Vegan Orange-Pecan Cornmeal Waffles

By Moriah Lindsley

Ingredients

The Night Before:
1/3 C dry soybeans, chickpeas, or white beans
Plenty of water to cover

In the Morning:
1 1/2 C fresh water
1/3 C orange juice concentrate
1 C cornmeal
2 T oat bran (or rice bran or wheat bran)
4 tsp sugar (or another sweetener)
2 T ground golden flaxseeds
1 T grated (organic) orange zest
1/2 T baking powder
1 tsp pure vanilla extract
1/2 tsp baking soda
3/4 tsp salt
1/3 C finely chopped toasted pecans

Instructions

The Night Before:
1. Soak the beans in plenty of water to cover, with a few inches of water above the beans.
Note: The beans can soak in water in the refrigerator for up to 1 week with no fermentation, if you aren't sure when you'll make the waffles.

In the Morning:
1. Drain the beans. Place in a blender along with all the other ingredients. Blend until smooth, light, and foamy.
2. Meanwhile, heat a nonstick waffle iron.* When hot, pour on a heaping 1/3 C of the batter for a 4-inch-square waffle. Close the iron and set the timer for 7 minutes. If the iron is hard to open when the time's up, let the waffle cook for a couple more minutes. The waffle should be brown and crispy. Repeat until all the batter has been used. If the batter gets thicker while standing, add a little water, just until it's the consistency you started out with.
3. Serve immediately, or let cool on cake racks. Serve with your favorite toppings. Leftovers can be place in the freezer in plastic bags or rigid containers.
4. If you don't use all the batter and you don't want to cook the waffles ahead of time and freeze them, just refrigerate the batter in the blender with the lid on. You can re-blend the batter (adding a tiny bit more water if the batter has become too thick) just before cooking.

*Note: Even if you have a nonstick iron, this recipe will work better if you spray the grids well with oil from a pump-sprayer.

Makes 8 waffles
