Forkinit: Vietnamese Coconut Soy Curls

By barbara.shaurette
The recipe this was adapted from featured pork as its main ingredient - I've been experimenting and had some good luck lately with getting a pork-like flavor out of my soy curls, using a combination of seasoned instant gravy and liquid smoke/Bragg's aminos. This meal is really rich - be sure to have some steamed vegetables or a salad on the side.

Ingredients

1 8-oz. bag of Butler Soy Curls
8 tsp Bisto Gravy Granules
3 cups water
1 tsp liquid smoke
1 tsp Bragg's Liquid Aminos
2-3 Tbsp sesame oil

1/2 tsp ground black pepper
1/2 tsp kosher salt
5 cloves garlic, finely minced
1 large shallot, finely minced
2 Tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
1 tsp sugar
1/2 tsp ground ginger
1/2 tsp Chinese 5 spice
1/4 tsp red pepper flakes
juice of 1/2 a lemon
1 can low-fat coconut milk

rice, for serving
steamed vegetables, for serving
scallions, sliced thinly, for garnish

Instructions

To prepare the soy curls: Empty the bag of soy curls into a wide, shallow bowl (like a small casserole dish). Boil 3 cups of water, mix it with the Bisto, liquid smoke, Bragg's aminos, and pour over the soy curls. Let the mixture soak for about 10 minutes.

Drain the soy curls. Heat the sesame oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add in the soy curls, toss with salt and pepper, and cook until slightly browned.

Combine the minced garlic, minced shallot, soy sauce, granulated sugar, ground ginger, Chinese 5 spice, red pepper flakes, and lemon juice in a bowl, then pour over the browned soy curls. Stir to combine, then add in the coconut milk and stir to combine. Cover, reduce the heat to medium-low, and cook for about 30 minutes, stirring every so often to prevent burning.

Remove the lid of the pot, stir, and allow to cook for another 30 minutes or so, allowing the sauce to reduce a bit. Taste and adjust seasoning as necessary. Serve over rice and garnish with a few thinly sliced scallions.

Yields: 8
Prep time: 1-1/2 hours
