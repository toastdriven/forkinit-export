Forkinit: Corn and Black Bean Empanadas

By barbara.shaurette

Ingredients

CRUST
1-1/2 cups corn flour
1 cup all-purpose flour
1-1/2 tsp. salt
1/2 tsp. chili powder
4 Tbs. cold vegan margarine, cut into 1/2-inch cubes
1/2 cup unsweetened applesauce
1 Tbs. white wine vinegar

FILLING
1 Tbs. olive oil
1 cup cooked black beans
1 clove garlic, minced (1 tsp.)
2 cups frozen corn
1 tsp. ground cumin
1/2 tsp. cayenne pepper
1/4 tsp. ground coriander
1 tsp. red pepper sauce, such as Tabasco

Instructions

1. To make Crust: Sift flours, salt, and chili powder into a bowl. Mix in margarine with fingers until mixture resembles coarse meal. Whisk together applesauce, vinegar, and 1/3 cup cold water in a separate bowl. Stir applesauce mixture into flour mixture until texture dough forms, adding up to 1/4 cup more water if necessary. Knead on lightly floured surface until dough comes together. Form into ball, wrap in plastic wrap, and chill 1 hour, or overnight.

2. To make Filling, heat oil in large skillet over medium-high heat. Add beans, corn and garlic, reduce heat to medium, and cook 3 minutes. Stir in cumin, cayenne, and coriander, and cook 2 minutes. Remove from heat, and stir in red pepper sauce.

3. Preheat oven to 400F. Divide dough into 12 balls. Coat baking sheet with cooking spray. Roll out each ball to 6-inch round (1/4-inch thick) on lightly floured work surface. Fill with approx. 3 Tbs. filling and brush edges of pastry with water. Fold dough circle in half, press to close, trim excess dough and crimp edges with fork to seal.

4. Transfer to prepared baking sheet, and repeat with remaining dough balls and filling. Chill 10 minutes. Brush a little oil on top of the pastries before baking to give them some crunch. Bake empanadas for 20 minutes, or until golden brown and crusty. Cool 5 minutes on baking sheet before serving.

Yields: 6
