Forkinit: Whole Wheat Bread Loaf

By barbara.shaurette
Makes enough dough for at least four 1-pound loaves. The recipe is easily doubled or halved. My instructions are for a stand mixer, but you can just as easily mix by hand.

Ingredients

7 cups whole wheat flour
1-1/2 Tbsp granulated yeast (or 2 packets)
1 Tbsp salt (increase or decrease to taste)
1/4 cup vital wheat gluten
3-3/4 cups lukewarm water

Instructions

Whisk together the flour, yeast, salt and vital wheat gluten in a mixing bowl.

Add the water and mix without kneading, using a dough hook on the stand mixer.

Drape the mixing bowl with a clean cotton cloth and allow the dough to rest at room temperature until it rises and collapses, approximately 2 hours.

The dough can be used right away, but it's easier to handle when cold. Refrigerate it in a lidded (not airtight) container and use it over the next 10 days.

On baking day:

Dust the surface of the refrigerated dough with flour and cut off a 1-pound (grapefruit-sized) piece - this should be 1/4 of the dough if you've made the full batch. Dust the piece with more flour and quickly shape it into a ball by stretching the surface of the dough around to the bottom on all four sides, rotating the ball a quarter turn as you go.

Allow the loaf to rest, loosely covered with plastic wrap, on a baking tray lined with a silicone mat, for 90 minutes (40 minutes if you're using fresh, unrefrigerated dough).

Thirty minutes before baking, preheat the oven to 450F. Place an empty broiler tray or other deep baking tray on the bottom rack of the oven.

Just before baking, use a pastry brush to paint the top of the loaf with water. Use a serrated bread knife to slash the top of the loaf with 1/4-inch-deep parallel cuts.

Slide the baking tray into the oven. Pour 2 cups of tap water into the heated broiler tray and quickly close the oven door.

Bake for about 30-35 minutes. Allow the bread to cool on a rack before slicing and eating.

Yields: 4 loaves
Prep time: 15 mins
