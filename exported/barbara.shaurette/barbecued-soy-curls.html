Forkinit: Barbecued Soy Curls

By barbara.shaurette
These are great served over rice or quinoa, with a little broccoli or something else green on the side.

Ingredients

3 cups nearly boiling water
1 tablespoon chicken-flavored vegetarian bouillon granules
1 teaspoon poultry seasoning
4 ounces dehydrated Soy Curls
1 to 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
1/2 teaspoon garlic granules
Freshly ground black pepper
3/4 cup bottled barbecue sauce
Salt

Instructions

In a large mixing bowl, blend nearly boiling water with bouillon and
poultry seasoning until bouillon has dissolved. Add dehydrated Soy
Curls and, using the back of a large spoon, press into the water
mixture until they are all covered. Let Curls sit for 10 minutes.
Drain them in a colander, pressing them firmly with your hands or the
back of a large spoon to squeeze out as much excess water as possible.

Heat a large cast-iron skillet over medium heat, 3 to 4 minutes. Add 1 tablespoon vegetable oil, shaking the pan until the entire cooking
surface is lightly coated. When oil starts to shimmer add the Soy
Curls, distributing them evenly around the pan so that they brown
evenly and don't just steam.

Cook Soy Curls 5 to 7 minutes, using a spatula to turn them every few
minutes. As Curls cook, sprinkle with garlic granules and a little
pepper. If pan starts to look dry and Curls begin sticking, add some
of the remaining 1 tablespoon vegetable oil, tossing the Curls to
evenly distribute the oil. Add barbecue sauce, tossing Curls to coat
evenly. Continue cooking 1 to 2 minutes more, until sauce begins to
caramelize but not burn. Taste the Curls and season with additional
salt and pepper, if needed.

Yields: 4-6
Prep time: 15 min
