Forkinit: Vegan Cottage Pie

By barbara.shaurette
Interesting note: Cottage and Shepherd's Pie are essentially the same thing - ground meat and some vegetables under a crust of mashed potatoes. It's only called Shepherd's Pie when it's made with ground lamb.

Ingredients

For the potatoes:
1-1/2 pounds russet potatoes
1/4 cup almond milk
2 Tbsp Earth Balance margarine
3/4 teaspoon kosher salt
1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
1 egg equivalent (1-1/2 tsp Ener-G powder + 2 Tbsp water)

For the filling:
2 Tbsp Earth Balance margarine
2 cloves garlic, minced
1 12 oz. pkg Lightlife Smart Ground, Original
1 teaspoon kosher salt
1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
1 cup vegetable broth
1 Tbsp Bisto gravy granules
1 teaspoon vegan Worcestershire sauce
1/2 teaspoon dried crushed rosemary
1/2 teaspoon dried thyme leaves
1 package frozen mixed vegetables

Instructions

For the potatoes:
Peel the potatoes and cut into 1/2-inch dice. Place in a saucepan, cover with water, bring to a boil over high heat. Simmer until fork tender, approximately 10 to 15 minutes. Drain the potatoes and then return to the
saucepan. Mash the potatoes with the almond milk, margarine, salt and pepper until smooth. Stir in the egg substitute until well combined.

Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F.

While the potatoes are cooking, prepare the filling. Melt margarine in a 12-inch saute pan over medium high heat. Add the (fake) ground beef, salt and pepper and cook until browned and cooked through, approximately 3 minutes. Add the frozen vegetables and continue to cook, stirring constantly, for another 3 minutes. Add the garlic and stir to combine. Add the vegetable broth, Worcestershire, rosemary, thyme, Bisto and stir to combine. Bring to a boil, reduce the heat to low, cover and simmer slowly 5-8 minutes or until the sauce is thickened slightly.

Spread the meat mixture evenly into an 11x7-inch glass baking dish, or a round pie pan. Using a rubber spatula, smooth the mashed potatoes over the top, starting around the edges to create a seal to prevent the mixture from bubbling up. Once the dish is completely covered, run a fork across the top to draw ridges in the potatoes.

Bake for approximately 25 minutes (or just until the potatoes begin to brown and crisp). Cool for about 10 minutes before serving.

Source: adapted from an Alton Brown recipe
