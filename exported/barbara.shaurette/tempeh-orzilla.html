Forkinit: Tempeh Orzilla

By barbara.shaurette

Ingredients

8 oz orzo

For the tempeh
8 oz tempeh
2 tablespoons sesame oil
1/2 teaspoon dried coriander
1/2 teaspoon anise seed
1 tablespoon soy sauce

For the sauce:
2 teaspoons olive oil
1 small red onion, thinly sliced
4 cloves garlic, minced
1/2 cup sundried tomatoes (dry ones, not the oil packed kind)
1/2 teaspoon dried rosemary
1 cup dry white wine wine
1/2 teaspoon salt
Fresh black pepper
1 cup vegetable broth
2 tablespoons nutritional yeast
3 cups baby spinach

Instructions

Put the tempeh in a shallow bowl along with the sesame oil, coriander, anise, and soy sauce. Cover and let marinate for about 20 minutes.

Preheat a small pan over medium heat. Saute tempeh in the oil and spices from the marinade, mashing the tempeh into crumbles with your spatula as you go. After about 10 minutes turn off the heat and cover until ready to use.

Boil orzo until al dente, then drain and set aside.

Preheat a large pan over medium heat. Saute the onion in olive oil with a pinch of salt until soft, about 5 minutes. Add garlic and saute for 30 seconds more.

Add rosemary, wine and sundried tomatoes. Season with salt and pepper and turn the heat up to bring the sauce to a simmer. Let simmer until reduced by about half, 5 minutes or so.

Add broth and yeast and warm through. Then add the spinach in handfuls, letting each batch wilt before adding the next. Cook until spinach is thoroughly wilted then turn the heat off, add the orzo, mix well, and serve topped with tempeh crumbles.

Yields: 6
Prep time: 30 mins
