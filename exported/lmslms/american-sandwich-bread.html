Forkinit: American Sandwich Bread

By fasola4mi
Adapted from Baking Illustrated, this bread uses white whole wheat flour and is not too sweet, as some whole wheat breads can be.

Ingredients

3-ish cups of white whole wheat flour
2 tsp salt
1 cup buttermilk, or yogurt thinned to buttermilk consistency with whey, milk, or water
1/3 c. boiling water
2 tbs. unsalted butter, melted
2 tbs. honey
2 1/4 tsp honey

Instructions

1. Oil dough hook, bowl, and pan (9 inches long by 5 inches wide).
2. Mix 3 cups flour and the salt in standing mixer fitted with oiled dough hook.
3. Melt butter, mix boiling water with yogurt, and mix together in 4 c. measuring cup. Mix in honey. Test temperature. It should be about 110 degrees. When at the right temperature, mix in yeast.
4. Turn mixer to low and slowly add liquid. When it begins to come together, increase speed to medium. After 5 minutes, if dough is still sticky, add flour by the tablespoon until the dough is no longer sticky. Knead for about 10 minutes total.
5. Turn dough onto lightly floured board, knead for about 15 seconds to form a round smooth ball.
6. Place the dough in the lightly oiled bowl, turn around to oil all the ball, and cover bowl. place in a warm place to rise for 1 hour.
7. Press the dough into a rectangle 1 inch thick and 9 inches long. Roll the dough into a long cylinder, pressing to be sure dough seals to itself. Pinch seam closed. Place seam side down in the pan, press so that it touches all sides. Cover, place in warm place to double in size, 20-30 minutes.
8. Place one rack on the bottom level of the oven, one rack in the middle. Preheat to 350 degrees. Place a baking pan on the lowest rack. When loaf is ready to be placed in the oven, fill the pan with about 2 cups of boiling water. Place loaf on middle rack. Bake for 40-50 minutes, until internal temperature of loaf is 195.
9. Remove bread from pan, cool on a wire rack until the loaf is completely cool.
This bread keeps for at least a week, and also freezes well.

Yields: 1 nine inch loaf
Prep time: 3 hours
Source: Baking Illustrated
