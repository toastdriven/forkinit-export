Forkinit: Honey-Soy Broiled Salmon

By Megan Swicegood
Sweet, tangy an salty fish dish. 161 calories; 5 g fat; 5 g carbohydrate; 23 g protien

Ingredients

2 tablespoons reduced-sodium soy sauce
1 tablespoon rice vinegar
1 tablespoon honey
1 scallion, minced
1 teaspoon minced fresh ginger
1 pound center-cut salmon fillet, preferably wild Pacific skinned and cut into 4 portions
1 teaspoon toasted sesame seeds

Instructions

1. Whisk soy sauce, vinegar, honey, scallion and ginger in a medium bowl until the honey is dissolved. Place salmon in a sealable plastic bag, add 3 tablespoons of the sauce and refrigerate; let marinate for 15 minutes. Reserve the remaining sauce.
2. Preheat broiler. Line a small baking pan with foil and coat with cooking spray.
3. Transfer the salmon to the pan, skinned-side down. (Discard the marinade.) Broil the salmon 4 to 6 inches from the heat source until cooked through, 6 to 10 minutes. Drizzle with the reserved sauce and garnish with sesame seeds.

Yields: 4 servings
Prep time: 20 minutes active, 40 minutes total
