Forkinit: Turkey Meatball Grinders

By johnjmonaghaniii
Zesty meatballs with marinara and provolone. Also great as a meatloaf. Just spread on the marinara after 30 minutes in the oven at 350 degrees, cook for 10 more minutes, add provolone slices, and broil till done.

Ingredients

1/3 of an Onion
1/2 of a Medium Carrot
1/3 of a Red Bell Pepper
3 Cloves of Garlic peeled
1/2 C Panko Breadcrumbs
1/4 tsp Black Pepper
1/4 tsp Cayenne
1/4 tsp Chili Powder
1/4 tsp Dried Basil
1/4 tsp Dried Thyme
1/4 tsp Kosher Salt
2 tsp Worcestershire Sauce
1 Egg
1 lb Ground Turkey
Hoagie buns
Provolone cheese slices
Marinara sauce



Instructions

Add peeled garlic to food processor and pulse a few times. Then add Onion, Carrot, and Red Bell Pepper to food processor and pulse until finely minced, but not into a paste.

Mix together breadcrumbs, black pepper, cayenne, chili powder, dried basil, dried thyme, and kosher salt in a bowl.

Add ground turkey to a bowl and lightly pull apart with a fork.
Pour vegetable and breadcrumb mixture over the ground turkey.

Whisk together egg and worcestershire, add to turkey mixture, and gently mix together.

Roll into one inch meatballs and place onto a broiler pan.
Note: Wetting one's hands makes rolling meatballs a snap.

Broil meatballs 6 inches from heat source for 6-8 minutes. Turn meatballs and continue to broil for an additional 3-4 minutes or until done.

Heat marinara sauce in a large skillet.
Transfer meatballs into marinara. Cover and cook for 10-15 minutes

Assemble sandwiches on a cookie sheet or reuse the broiler pan:
Slice hoagie rolls open
Ladel a small amount of sauce into each roll
Add meatballs
Lay provolone slices over the top of the meatballs
Place back under the broiler for a few minutes to melt cheese and toast rolls.



Yields: 4 sandwiches
Prep time: 45 minutes
