Forkinit: Crock Pot Carnitas

By johnjmonaghaniii
A house favorite that never disappoints. Goes great with Mexican grilled "street" corn which also uses cotija cheese.
When it comes to leftovers, this pork is very versatile. Goes great with eggs, salads, and reheats well for sandwiches or more tacos.

Ingredients

1 Orange (sliced in half)
3-4lbs Pork Shoulder Roast
1 T Chile Powder
2 t Cumin Powder
1.5 t Garlic Powder
1 T Salt
1/2 t Chipotle Chile Powder
1/2 t Pepper
1/2 t Oregano


1 Red Onion
1/2 cup Cotija Cheese
Corn Tortillas
1 bunch Cilantro
3 Limes

Instructions

Mix spices together in a bowl
Squeeze one half of the orrange into the dry spices and mix to make a soupy paste
Juice the other half of the orange into the bottom of the crock pot
Place Pork in crock pot
Pour/rub the spice paste all over the pork
Place the spent orrange halves into the crock pot with the pork
Cook on high for 1.5 hours
Cook on Low for 6-8 hours
Preheat oven to 400
Remove pork to a large baking sheet and shred using two forks
Spread shredded pork out evenly on the pan and place in the oven
Cook for 10 minutes
While pork is cooking, pour all the delicious juices from the crock pot through a strainer into a small sauce pot
Bring to a boil and reduce heat to a strong simmer (medium heat). Simmer until reduced to about 1/2 - 3/4 cup. Set aside
After pork has cooked for 10 min, use tongs to toss/stir and re-spread the pork.
Return pork to the oven and cook for an additional 10 minutes.
Pour sauce/juices from the sauce pot over the pork. Mix and re-spread the pork
Return pork to the oven for 5 minutes
Remove pork from the oven, juice a lime over thee pork and toss to coat

Serve with:
Warm corn tortilla
Grated cotija cheese
Minced red onion
Chopped cilantro
Lime wedges

Yields: 20 tacos
Prep time: 10 min cook prep, 8 hr unattended cook, 25 min serving prep
